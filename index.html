Condistiller An
E-Waste Based Water Purification System.

Condistiller is a low-cost water purification and testing system designed using recycled electronic waste. The project combines a bottle-based water distillation unit with a conductivity and TDS measurement module to evaluate water quality efficiently. It demonstrates how discarded electronic components can be repurposed into functional scientific tools for educational and environmental applications.

About CONDISTILLERstats

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Condistiller is an integrated water purification and quality assessment system developed using recycled electronic waste. The project addresses the growing challenge of water contamination in Egypt by combining a low-cost distillation unit with an electronic conductivity and TDS measurement module.

The system consists of two main components: a bottle-based water distillation setup that converts tap water into distilled water, and a conductivity meter constructed from salvaged electronic parts to estimate total dissolved solids (TDS). This approach demonstrates the practical reuse of e-waste in building functional scientific devices for educational and laboratory applications.

PROJECT DEVELOPMENT TIMELINE

2025 – Initial Phase

PROJECT CONCEPT - RESEARCH

Initial research was conducted to study water purification challenges in Egypt and the potential reuse of electronic waste. Distillation principles, water conductivity, and TDS concepts were reviewed to define the project scope and objectives.

2025 – Design Phase

SYSTEM DESIGN - MATERIAL SELECTION

The system design was finalized, including a bottle-based water distillation unit and a conductivity measurement module. Low-cost and recycled materials extracted from electronic waste were selected to ensure sustainability and affordability.

2025 – Assembly Stage

DISTILLATION UNIT CONSTRUCTION

A functional water distiller was assembled using reused plastic containers, a recovered heating element, silicone tubing, and a small pump. The setup enabled efficient evaporation and condensation of tap water into distilled water.

2025 – Circuit Implementation

CONDUCTIVITY METER DEVELOPMENT

A conductivity meter was developed using a recycled integrated circuit, reference resistor, and basic wiring. The IC was programmed to process voltage readings and estimate EC and TDS values accurately.

2025 – Experimental Phase

CALIBRATION - TESTING

Calibration was performed using standard NaCl solutions and a laboratory EC/TDS meter. Multiple water samples, including tap water and distilled water, were tested to validate accuracy, reproducibility, and error percentage.

2025 – Final Phase

RESULTS - EVALUATION

The system successfully reduced TDS levels through distillation, with conductivity readings showing an error of approximately ±2%. Results confirmed that recycled electronic components can reliably support water quality testing.

What is Condistiller?

Condistiller is a low-cost system that combines water distillation and conductivity measurement to evaluate water purity using recycled electronic components.

Why is water distillation important?

Water distillation removes dissolved salts and impurities, improving water quality and making it suitable for laboratory and educational use.

How does the distillation unit work?

Tap water is heated until evaporation occurs, and the produced vapor is condensed into distilled water using a cooling system.

How is water purity measured?

Water purity is evaluated by measuring electrical conductivity and converting it into Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) values.

What role does e-waste play in the project?

Electronic waste components were reused to build the conductivity meter, reducing cost and promoting environmental sustainability.

What were the final results?

The system successfully reduced TDS levels after distillation, with measurement accuracy maintained within an error range of ±2%.
